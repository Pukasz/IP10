# Educational Website Wireframe - Design Summary

## Website Purpose
An educational platform helping young children improve reading comprehension and communication skills, accessible via web browser.

---

## Page Structure & Sections

### 1. **Header/Navigation**
- **Brand Logo**: "ReadFlow" (or your preferred name)
- **Navigation Menu**: Links to main sections
  - Why (About/Mission)
  - How It Helps (Benefits)
  - Practice Test (Assessment)
  - Contact
- **Design**: Dark, professional header with high contrast

### 2. **Hero Section (Landing)**
- **Large Headline**: Attention-grabbing statement about the benefits
- **Subheading**: Brief value proposition
- **Primary CTA Button**: "Start Your Practice Test" (prominent, contrasting color)
- **Design**: Gradient background (purple/blue theme), large typography, centered

### 3. **Why Section** - Explains the mission
#### Content:
- **Problem Statement**: Why reading comprehension matters
- **The Challenge**: Common struggles children face
- **Our Mission**: What the website aims to solve
- **Design**: Clean white background, readable text, good spacing

### 4. **How It Helps Section** - Benefits overview
#### 6 Benefit Cards in a grid:
1. **Targeted Practice** - Focused exercises with personalized feedback
2. **Progress Tracking** - Detailed insights and reports
3. **Engaging & Fun** - Interactive, game-like approach
4. **Learn at Your Pace** - No pressure, flexible timing
5. **Parental Insights** - Reports for parents/guardians
6. **Evidence-Based** - Developed by education specialists

**Design**: 3-column grid on desktop, 1-column on mobile, card format with icons/color accents

### 5. **Practice Test Section**
#### Left Side - Description:
- Explanation of what's included
- Features list:
  - Reading Passages with comprehension questions
  - Vocabulary Exercises
  - Communication Tasks
  - Instant Feedback
- **Secondary CTA**: "Take the Practice Test Now"

#### Right Side - Sample Question Preview:
- Live example of a reading passage
- Sample multiple-choice question
- Shows how feedback would appear
- **Design**: Light gray background with white preview box, shows actual test experience

### 6. **Getting Started Section**
#### 3-Step Process:
1. **Step 1: Sign Up** - Quick account creation (2 minutes)
2. **Step 2: Take Practice Test** - Establish baseline
3. **Step 3: Start Learning** - Personalized exercises
- **CTA Button**: "Create Free Account"
- **Design**: Card layout, numbered steps, encouraging tone

### 7. **Footer**
- Copyright information
- Links to Terms, Privacy, Contact
- Tagline/mission reminder
- **Design**: Dark background (matches header), white text

---

## Key Design Principles

1. **Child-Friendly Yet Professional**
   - Bright, engaging colors (purples, blues, orange accents)
   - Clear, easy-to-read fonts
   - Sufficient whitespace

2. **Parents as Secondary Audience**
   - Professional design instills trust
   - Clear explanations of benefits
   - Emphasis on progress tracking and insights

3. **Call-to-Action Strategy**
   - Multiple CTAs encourage exploration: "Start Practice Test", "Create Free Account"
   - Primary CTA (orange/yellow) stands out
   - Buttons placed strategically at end of persuasive sections

4. **Information Architecture**
   - Clear narrative flow: Why → How It Helps → Try It
   - Sections build case for using the platform
   - Practice test is the central feature, not hidden

5. **Responsive Design**
   - Mobile-friendly layout
   - Grids adapt from 3 columns → 1 column on small screens
   - Touch-friendly buttons and spacing

---

## User Flow

1. **First-Time Visitor** lands on homepage
2. Reads "Why" section → understands the value
3. Browses "How It Helps" → sees specific benefits
4. Views practice test example → understands what they'll get
5. Signs up or takes the practice test
6. (Future) Accesses dashboard with personalized lessons
